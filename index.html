0/1 Kanpsack Problem

A thief robbing a safe finds it filled with N types of items of varying size and value, but has only a small knapsack of capacity M to use to carry the goods. More precisely, the knapsack problem is to find the combination of items which the thief should choose for his knapsack in order to maximize the total value of all the items he takes. It is called 0/1 since the items only can be taken as whole not a fraction. For example, suppose that he has a knapsack of capacity 17 and the safe contains many items of each of the sizes and values shown in the figure below. (Here, we use single-letter names for the items in the example and integers in the program.) Then the thief can take five A’s (but not six) for total of 20, or he can fill up his knapsack with a D and an E for a total of 24, or he can try many other combinations. But which will maximize his total take?
